# $\fbox{Module 3: MAGNETIC CIRCUITS}$

> **Purpose**  
> Understand how magnetic fields store and guide energy in electrical machines and transformers, using magnetic-circuit analogies.

---

## 3.1 Why Magnetic Circuits Matter

#### Electrical machines work by:

- Converting **electrical energy ↔ magnetic energy ↔ mechanical energy**

Magnetic circuits provide a **controlled path for magnetic flux**, just like conductors guide current.

---

## 3.2 Magnetic Flux ($\Phi$)

- Symbol: $\Phi$
- Unit: **Weber ($Wb$)**

Magnetic flux represents the **total magnetic field passing through an area**.

---

## 3.3 Magnetomotive Force (MMF)

MMF is the cause of magnetic flux.

$$ \text{MMF} = NI $$

#### Where:

- $N$ = number of turns
- $I$ = current ($A$)

Unit: **Ampere-turn ($AT$)**

---

## 3.4 Magnetic Field Intensity ($H$)

- Symbol: **$H$**
- Unit: **$A/m$**

Represents the **strength of the magnetic field** produced by MMF.

---

## 3.5 Magnetic Flux Density ($B$)

- Symbol: **$B$**
- Unit: **Tesla ($T$)**

$$ B = \frac{\Phi}{A} $$

Represents **flux per unit area**.

---

## 3.6 Permeability ($\mu$)

Permeability measures how easily a material supports magnetic flux.

$$ \mu = \mu_0 \mu_r $$

#### Where:

- ($\mu_0$) = permeability of free space
- ($\mu_r$) = relative permeability

High $\mu$ → easier flux flow.

---

## 3.7 Reluctance ($\mathcal{R}$)

Reluctance opposes magnetic flux.

$$ \mathcal{R} = \frac{l}{\mu A} $$

Analogous to resistance in electric circuits.

---

## 3.8 Magnetic Circuit Law (Hopkinson’s Law)

$$ \Phi = \frac{\text{MMF}}{\mathcal{R}} $$

#### Direct analog of Ohm’s law:

|Electrical|Magnetic|
|---|---|
|Voltage|MMF|
|Current|Flux|
|Resistance|Reluctance|

---

## 3.9 Series and Parallel Magnetic Circuits

- Series: same flux, MMF drops add
- Parallel: same MMF, flux divides

Used in machine cores and transformers.

---

## 3.10 Magnetic Energy Storage

Energy stored in magnetic field:

$$ W = \frac{1}{2} LI^2 $$

Magnetic fields **store and return energy**, not dissipate it.

---

## 3.11 Hysteresis and Core Losses

Real magnetic materials exhibit:

- **Hysteresis** → energy loss per cycle
- **Eddy currents** → circulating currents causing heat

These losses matter in AC machines.

---

## 3.12 Summary (Module 3)

- MMF drives magnetic flux
- Reluctance opposes flux
- Magnetic circuits parallel electrical circuits
- Core losses limit efficiency
