# $$ \fbox{Introduction} $$





This book covers some of the most popular typology systems like *Classic Jungian Typology* (from *Psychological Types* by *Carl Jung*), from where modern typology systems like *Myers Briggs Type Indicator* (*MBTI*) and *Socionics* by *Aušra Augustinavičiūtė* were derived. Then the original *Enneagram* theory by *Oscar Ichazo* and their subtypes given by his student *Claudio Naranjo* in his clinical observations. Then the *4 Temperaments* by *Galen*. Then *Psychosophy* by *Aleksandr Afanasyev*. Then the *Moral Alignment* by *Gary Gygax* & *Dave Arneson*. And then finally the *Big 5* (*Five-Factor Model*) by *Paul Costa* & *Robert McCrae*.

If I have to list them down here in order of discussion (more systems might be added later on):
1. Classic Jungian Typology (by *Carl Jung*)
2. Enneagram Theory (by *Oscar Ichazo* & *Claudio Naranjo*)
3. 4 Temperaments (by *Galen*)
4. Psychosophy (by *Aleksandr Afanasyev*)
5. Moral Alignment (by *Gary Gygax* & *Dave Arneson*)
6. Big 5 (by *Paul Costa* & *Robert McCrae*)

For each listed system, I will be explaining about what the original intent of the author was, what terminologies they use, how the primitives join to produce core theory, what the modern misconceptions are, and finally why not to spend time in endless inquiry about them. Our objective will be to understand the original theory, popular misconceptions about them, their limitation in use cases, and why not to obsess over them. As simple and short as that.

And as far this book is concerned, it won't be released all in once but in incremental integration of each system. And it might expand to more systems in future.
