## **Chapter - 1: Matrices & Determinants**

- **Trace ($tr(A)$):** Sum of diagonal elements in matrix.
- **Symmetric matrix:** $A=A^T$
- **Skew-symmetric matrix:** $A=-A^T$
- **Singular matrix:** Determinant of matrix gives $0$
- **Identity matrix ($I$):** Diagonal elements are $1$, rest $0$.
- **Orthogonal matrix:** $AA^T=I$
- **Unique solution:** Two lines intersect (consistent)
- **No solution:** Two lines parallel (inconsistent)
- **Infinitely many sols:** Two lines coincide (consistent)
- **Augmented matrix:** Linear equations as matrix.
- **Unique sol matrix:** One full $0$ row $+$ Constant non-zero
- **Infinitely many sols matrix:** One full $0$ row $+$ Constant also zero
- **Unique sol matrix:** Every row has leading $1$.
- Matrices are made echelon before categorized into any 3.
- **Leading elements:** Elements making up diagonal.
- **Row echelon (RE):** Leading $1$s $+$ Lower half only $0$s $+$ Full $0$ rows at last
- **Reduced row echelon (RRE):** Leading $1$s $+$ Rest are $0$s
- **Gauss elimination:** RE is written as linear equations & variables found.
- **Gauss Jordan:** Gauss elimination $+$ Introducing extra variable to solve
- **Homogeneous eq/ trivial sol:** All variables are $0$ ($AX=0$).
- **Matrix rank ($r$ or $p(A)$):** Dimension of determinant subset with non-zero value.
- **$AX=\lambda X$:** $\lambda$ is eigen value & $X$ is eigen vector.
- **Characteristic eq:** $|A- \lambda I_n|=0$
- **Algebraic multiplicity:** Number of equal eigen values as roots.
- **Geometry multiplicity:** Number of $0$ eigen values.
- **Eigen space:** Reverse of augmented matrix.
- **Caley-Hamilton theorem:** Matrix represented as its own eigen value (all leadings $-\lambda$).
- **Inverse of matrix ($A^{-1}$):** $\frac{1}{|A|}(A)$
- **Diagonalization:** $D=P^{-1}AP$ ($P$ is all eigen values rows put as matrix)
- **Canonical matrix form:** Quadratic eq as matrix (each variable on rows & columns).
