## I. Distribution Of Types

We will be discussing about psychological types in upcoming chapters, and the primitives they are formed from. But one very important fact to know is that the distribution of these types can't be categorized cleanly as homogeneous or heterogeneous. Distribution of types vary across the demographics of the world, and not dependent on a singular factor, but on sum of multiple factors. These factors may include default wiring of brain, early childhood experiences, and exposures to environment, etc. Out of these factors, the most determining factor is the default wiring of the brain, which is called the *tendency* of the individual. By default, an individual tends to do something, or process experiences in a particular manner dominantly, which hint strongly towards their types.

After early childhood phase is over, the decision upon what tendencies the individual will have is almost decided, with a few more gaps which is filled later by his future experiences. So whether they belong to a middle-class family, or an elite class family, or a family suffering from poverty doesn't erase their natural tendency to process events in a particular manner, though their circumstances may force them to adopt a different  attitude which doesn't match to their tendencies. This suppression, if used for prolonged duration, can make a quite hazardous psychological damages to a person.



## II. Going Against Tendency

People have access to other functions, and they can use them depending on the situation, or not use it as per their preference. They have complete autonomy to choose it or not choose if when a suitable situation arises. But its worth noting that their natural tendency still remains the same, and struggles to come up when a different function is used deliberately. A very relevant example to this behavior can be seen in highly conservative societies where children are forced to respect traditions, and seek perfection, even though their actual types have different tendencies than what is expected. Some might come up with tendency to scrutinize the traditional methods and practices, or question about what's believed to be taboo culturally. But because they know their environment is hostile towards their tendencies, and can punish them, they forcefully mask their true impulses. On the contrary, some might rebel in such environment and choose not to mask. So the decision to mask or not, depends partially on the individual.

Depending on the level of severity, if someone masks their true impulses, they cause psychological harm to themselves. By unconsciously masking or not masking, they avoided experiencing their true form, so those impulses when seen in an environment where they have freedom might come up as a shocking realization. Moreover, some cases of suppression could result in extremes. For instance, not being satisfied with life and thus relying on medications to cope with dissatisfaction is one form of doing so. And in some cases, even suicide.
