## **Topic - 1: Patterns / Varieties**

### <u>Letter Shifting</u>

- Letters are shifted by a fix number of letters.
- For example, $\text{APPLE} \rightarrow \text{BQQMF}$


### <u>Reversing & Shifting</u>

- Word is first reversed, then shifted by a fix number of letters.
- For example, $\text{APPLE} \rightarrow \text{ELPPA} \rightarrow \text{FMQQB}$


### <u>Opposite Letters</u>

- Letters are replaced with their polar opposite letters.
- Like $A \rightarrow Z$, $C \rightarrow X$, etc.
- For example, $\text{APPLE} \rightarrow \text{ZKKOV}$


### <u>Position Coding</u>

- Letters as per their sequence number & position are added, multiplied, joined, etc.
- For example, $\text{INDIA} \rightarrow \text{9 14 4 9 1} \rightarrow \text{914491}$
- Or, $\text{INDIA} \rightarrow \text{9+14+4+9+1} \rightarrow \text{37}$


### <u>Fictious Language</u>

- Words are represented by symbols.
- For example, $F \rightarrow \text{Apple}$, $S \rightarrow \text{Boy}$, etc.


### <u>Symbol Mixed Coding</u>

- Arithmetic symbols are interchanged with each other or represented differently.
- For example, $@ \rightarrow +$, $\# \rightarrow -$, etc.
