# $\fbox{JUNGIAN TYPOLOGY}$



## 1. Terminologies


- **Subject:** Centre of living conscious entity which can differentiate between subjective and objective factors.
- **Object:** The subjective or objective content in consciousness that the subject is focusing on at time.
- **Ego:** Awareness in center of conscious that identifies self as "I", and acts as mediator to experiences and consciousness & unconsciousness.
- **Suppressed:** Consciously holding back something.
- **Repressed:** Unconsciously blocked from awareness.
- **Irrational functions (S/N):** Functions which perceive reality without judging it.
- **Rational functions (T/F):** Function which perceive and judge reality.



## 2. Function Domains


### 2.1 <u>Sensing (S)</u>:

- Perceiving reality directly as immediate experience, in its concrete form.
- It registers perception of object in form of sensory stimulus.


### 2.2 <u>Intuition (N)</u>:

- Perceiving reality beyond sensory details in terms of what possibilities, meanings, or patterns underlies the object.
- It registers perception of object in form of what could emerge, what is implied, what would become, latent patterns, and future outcomes unconsciously.
- Its about what's not yet actual, but is possible.


### 2.3 <u>Thinking (T)</u>:

- Evaluates perceptions in terms of logical relations.
- It determines what object is by forming concepts, and making classifications.


### 2.4 <u>Feeling (F)</u>:

- Evaluates perceptions according to values.
- It determines whether something is acceptable or unacceptable, agreeable or disagreeable, worthy or unworthy.



## 3. Attitudes


### 3.1 <u>Introverted Functions (i)</u>:

- Orientation is directed primarily towards the subjective factor.
- Object is filtered through an inwardly determined standpoint that affects perception or judgement, instead of adapting to objective conditions.
- Resulting perception or judgement might appear detached or independent of collective standards.


### 3.2 <u>Extroverted Functions (e)</u>:

- Orientation is directed primarily towards the objective factor.
- Perception or judgement is governed by external conditions, observable facts, and collectively recognized values.
- Adaptation occurs through alignment with the outer situations.
- Resulting perception or judgement tends to be responsive, situational, and outwardly expressed.
- Its all about decisive reference point being external.



## 4. Functions With Attitudes


### 4.1 <u>Introverted Sensing (Si)</u>:

*"Awareness of internal bodily states and stored sensory impressions."*
(What is familiar?)

- Compares current experience with past experience
- Tracks comfort, familiarity, and internal reactions
- Prefers stability and known conditions


### 4.2 <u>Extroverted Sensing (Se)</u>:

*"Direct awareness of the physical environment as it is right now."*
(What is happening now?)

- Notices details, movement, opportunities for action
- Responds quickly to real-world changes
- Engages with what is tangible and immediate


### 4.3 <u>Introverted Intuition (Ni)</u>:

*"Forming a single, focused understanding of how things will unfold."*
(What will happen?)

- Synthesizes patterns into a clear internal insight
- Focuses on direction, timing, and outcomes
- Seeks the most likely future path


### 4.4 <u>Extroverted Intuition (Ne)</u>:

*"Seeing multiple possibilities and connections in the external world."*
(What could happen?)

- Generates ideas and alternatives rapidly
- Spots patterns, trends, and potential uses
- Moves from one possibility to another


### 4.5 <u>Introverted Thinking (Ti)</u>:

*"Building an internal system of logic to understand how things work."*
(What makes sense?)

- Focuses on internal consistency and precise reasoning
- Questions definitions, assumptions, and structure
- Wants things to _make sense logically_


### 4.6 <u>Extroverted Thinking (Te)</u>:

*"Organizing the external world using objective facts, rules, and measurable results."*
(What works?)

- Focuses on efficiency, evidence, and outcomes
- Trusts data, procedures, and proven methods
- Wants things to _work correctly in reality_


### 4.7 <u>Introverted Feeling (Fi)</u>:

*"Evaluating things based on personal, internal values."*
(What feels right?)

- Focuses on authenticity and personal ethics
- Forms deep but often unspoken value judgments
- Less concerned with group consensus


### 4.8 <u>Introverted Feeling (Fe)</u>:

*"Adjusting behavior based on shared emotional and social values."*
(What keeps harmony?)

- Tracks and responds to group emotions
- Expresses feelings outwardly
- Prioritizes harmony and social alignment



## 5. Positioning System


### 5.1 <u>Dominant (dom)</u>:

*"The function you rely on the most; it leads your perception and decisions automatically."*

- Most developed and trusted function
- Runs almost unconsciously (you don’t notice using it)
- Shapes how you interpret reality at a fundamental level
- Others tend to notice it more than you do


### 5.2 <u>Auxiliary (aux)</u>:

*"The second function that supports and stabilizes the dominant by providing a different mode of processing."*

- Helps correct the one-sidedness of the dominant
- Usually operates consciously and deliberately
- Adds flexibility and adaptability


### 5.3 <u>Tertiary (tert)</u>:

*"A weaker function that is partially developed and inconsistently used."*

- Less mature and often simplistic
- Can appear in bursts, especially in relaxed or playful states
- Not fully under conscious control


### 5.4 <u>Inferior (inf)</u>:

*"The least developed function, largely unconscious, and often a source of difficulty."*

- Hard to control and often avoided
- Becomes noticeable under stress or pressure
- Feels uncomfortable, uncertain, or overwhelming



## 6. Methodology - Life Simulation (v10.0.0)


### 6.1 <u>Procedure</u>:

- Pass information given below to *VRDW CJTE - 7* for simulating, along with the possible personality types to narrow down options.
- The given *simulation form* has to be filled up, with as specific information as possible, to make sure it goes in right direction.
- Attributes in form could be amended, removed, or modified, to cover user given information precisely.


### 6.2 <u>Definitions</u>:

- **Phase:** A unique name given to any part of life which uniquely isolates itself for lifestyle.
	- **Map:** A unique name given to the particular location subject was living at the time of phase.
	- **Submaps:** Part of maps, or places where the subject had to be at in a routine.
		- **Submap1:** A name given to the particular submap, more could be added with different name.
			- **Slice:** Time slice from whole phase timeline when the submap becomes applicable.
			- **Frequency:** Frequency in which the subplace had to be visited.
	- **Info:** Basic information about subject for relating the phase to maturity level.
		- **Age range:** Range of age which the phase covers.
		- **Occupation:** Occupation of the subject at the time which phase covers up.
		- **Illness:** Any special illness that was for a chronic period of time.
	- **Lifestyle:** Captures various lifestyle aspects of the user during runtime of phase.
		- **Routine:** General daily routine that subject followed during the phase.
		- **Facilities:** Exceptional facilities that the subject had at the time of phase.
		- **Scarcities:** Exceptional scarcities that the subject had at the time of phase.
	- **Environment:** General social atmosphere around the subject, including living conditions.
		- **Locality:** Tells what kind of locality was around subject, in one word or phrase.
		- **People:** Information what kind of interaction was occurring between subject and people regularly involved in phases.
			- **Guardian relation:** Relationship with whoever was the guardian of the subject at the time of phase.
			- **Sibling relation:** Relationship with biological siblings or cousins at the time.
			- **Friends relation:** Relationship with friends in general which the subject had at the time.
			- **Mentor relation:** Relationship with mentors in general at the time of phase (if any).
		- **Society:** Information about how society or general people around worked.
			- **Elements:** What the society consisted of, and who were the people involved.
			- **Societal values:** Values that the group of people around that formed society possessed.
	- **Moments:** Very special and notable moments that subject might remember about from the phase, whether experience was positive or negative.
		- **Situation:** Situation that occurred to make the moment notable, positively or negatively.
		- **Conclusion:** Conclusion that the situation being discussed reached.
