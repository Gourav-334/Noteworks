## **Topic - 2: Extroverted Types**

Extroverted type is linked with adaptation to objective factor. It mostly tries to adjust itself with the object naturally and neglects the subject until some serious unignorable sensation like disease rises or there comes need for compensation through introversion. Extroverted types are turned towards the real-world and they assimilate themselves with the object and finding abundance in it. And adjustment doesn't mean adaptation, which is weakness for extroverted types. They adjust to new object just like how they adapted to the previous object. There is little to no consideration for subjective factor until the compensation comes or serious bodily requirement rises.

## **Topic - 4: Perception & Judgement**

Judgement is concerned about the conscious reasons for psychic process, while perception is about registering the process itself. Some people might try to use their perception and judgement function with equal strength, blurring the line between extroversion and introversion of their attitude. So first step to determine someone's superior function isn't knowing if their attitude is introverted or extroverted, but to know which particular function they prefer consciously.



## **Topic - 5: Extraverted Thinking**

Extraverted thinking is concerned about the results, not the starting point or the process. It judges through data received from the objective factors at present moment than what is experienced personally throughout the lifetime in past. Even if subjective data and experience tends to explain something else, an extroverted thinking type looks for what is objectively established, directly visible, or clearly proven with results.

Extroversion and introversion can be confused when determining someone's type, as both involve subject and the object. A subjective type to make a judgement looks at its subjective data, but its inspiration or origin comes from the object. Same for objective types who have their inspiration originating from the object, but they use their subjective process to consciously process objective data. Thinking makes use of senses first to give birth to origin of an object to think about.

Its very common for extroverted thinking and introverted thinking types to have harsh opinions about each others. For instance, an extroverted thinking type may call out an introverted thinking type as too subjective. While an introverted thinking type may call out an extroverted thinking type as too uninteresting, dry, and rigid with results.
